# Beginner’s Manual: Your First Web3 Wallet (Self-Custodial)

To use Global South Digital Currency (GSDC), you need a **Web3 wallet**, not just a bank account. Think of it as both your digital passport and your digital vault.

## 1. What is a self-custodial wallet?

Consider this analogy:

- **Traditional banking** is like **valet parking**: you hand your keys (money) to the bank and trust them to manage it.  
- A **self-custodial wallet** is like a **private, high-security garage at your home**.  

Key properties:

- **You own the keys**: No government, bank, GSDC, or wallet provider can move your funds.  
- **No middleman**: There is no manager to approve transactions—you sign everything yourself.  
- **Total responsibility**: If you lose your keys, there is no reset or recovery by support.

## 2. The “master key”: seed phrase (12 or 24 words)

When you create a wallet, it generates a **Seed Phrase** or **Recovery Phrase** (12 or 24 random English words).

Why it matters:

- It is the **master key** to your funds across devices  
- Anyone with these words can fully control your money  
- It is the only way to recover your wallet if your phone or laptop is lost  

Best practices:

- Write it down on paper (not digitally) and store it securely  
- Never take photos, never send it via WhatsApp, and never store it in email/cloud notes  

<Warning>
  If you lose your seed phrase and your device breaks, your funds are permanently unrecoverable.
</Warning>

## 3. GSDC as an ERC-20 token

GSDC is implemented as a **smart contract** on the Ethereum blockchain using the ERC-20 token standard.

Implications:

- Any ERC-20 compatible wallet (like MetaMask) can hold GSDC  
- Transactions are transparent and verifiable on-chain  
- You can inspect transfers using explorers like Etherscan  

## 4. Gas fees and ETH

To move any token (including GSDC) on Ethereum, you must pay **gas fees** in ETH.

- Think of ETH as the **fuel or toll** that lets your “car” (GSDC) travel on the network  
- Without ETH, your wallet cannot submit transactions  

<Tip>
  Always maintain a small ETH balance in your wallet so you do not get stuck when you need to mint, redeem, or move GSDC.
</Tip>

## 5. Supported wallets

For beginners, we recommend:

- **MetaMask** (browser extension + mobile app): most widely used, compatible with GSDC  
- **Trust Wallet** (mobile-first): intuitive for users who prefer phones
