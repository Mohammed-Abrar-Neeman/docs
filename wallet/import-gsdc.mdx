# How to Import GSDC

Even if you already hold GSDC, your wallet may look empty until you import the GSDC token contract.

## Step 1: Open MetaMask and select Tokens

- Open the MetaMask app or extension  
- Ensure you are on **Ethereum Mainnet**  
- Navigate to the **Tokens** tab  

## Step 2: Open the import token flow

- Scroll to the bottom of your token list  
- Click **Import Tokens**  

## Step 3: Switch to the Custom Token tab

- MetaMask shows a search interface by default  
- Select the **Custom Token** tab to add GSDC manually  

## Step 4: Paste the official GSDC address

In **Token contract address**, paste:

```text
0xFf5559c92d3Ee2dAF4BC7F5C2F15e6eC5a82b1e4
```

## Step 4: Confirm token details

- The **Token Symbol** (GSDC) and **Token Decimal** should auto-fill.
- Click **Next** once the details appear.

## Step 5: Confirm import

- Review the token details.
- Click **Import**.
- You should see **GSDC** appear in your token list, along with your current balance.

## Essential reminders for Brazilian users

- Maintain enough **ETH** in the same wallet to cover future gas fees (especially for redemptions).
- Only use wallets that are **whitelisted** on the platform to mint or redeem.
- Make sure the wallet is operated by you and matches the identity (name and **CPF**) that passed KYC.

<Tip>
  If your GSDC balance does not appear after import, double-check that you used the correct network (Ethereum) and the official GSDC contract address.
</Tip>
